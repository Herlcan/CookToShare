Ube Halaya with Cheese

Published May 10, 2024

Halayang Ube (Ube Halaya) is a classic Filipino delicacy made of purple yam, coconut milk, and butter. Creamy, cheesy, and topped with golden latik, it's delicious as a snack or dessert.

This creamy and flavorful dessert combines the richness of ube (purple yam) with the creamy goodness of cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 500g purple yam (ube), peeled and cubed
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1 can (300ml) condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup grated cheese (cheddar or your favorite cheese)
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Steam the cubed purple yam until soft, about 20-25 minutes.
  2. Mash the steamed purple yam until smooth using a fork or potato masher. Set aside.
  3. In a saucepan, combine coconut milk, condensed milk, grated cheese, butter, granulated sugar, and salt. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture is well combined and slightly thickened.
  4. Add the mashed purple yam to the saucepan and continue cooking, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and starts to pull away from the sides of the pan.
  5. Transfer the ube halaya to a greased dish or container. Smooth out the top with a spatula.
  6. Let it cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 1 hour to set.
  7. Slice into squares or scoop into bowls to serve.
  8. Enjoy your delicious Ube Halaya with Cheese!
