Tapsilog

Published May 10, 2024

Tapsilog, a popular Filipino meal, is a portmanteau of the dish’s three components: tapa (beef), sinangag (garlic fried rice), and itlog (fried egg). It’s one of many variations of silog (garlic fried rice plus fried egg), others of which include tosilog (made with pork), bangsilog (fish), and Spamsilog (Spam).

Tapsilog is a popular Filipino dish consisting of Tapa (marinated beef), Sinangag (garlic fried rice), and Itlog (fried egg).

Ingredients:

  • For the Tapa:
    • 500g beef sirloin, thinly sliced
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
    • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon vinegar
    • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
    • Salt and pepper to taste
  • For the Sinangag:
    • 4 cups cooked rice
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
    • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    • Salt to taste
  • For the Itlog:
    • 4 eggs
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the thinly sliced beef sirloin with minced garlic, soy sauce, vinegar, brown sugar, salt, and pepper. Mix well and marinate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight.
  2. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Add the marinated beef and cook until browned and caramelized, about 5-7 minutes. Set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and cook until golden brown and fragrant, about 1-2 minutes.
  4. Add the cooked rice to the skillet and stir-fry until heated through and lightly toasted, about 5 minutes. Season with salt to taste.
  5. Meanwhile, in another skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Crack eggs into the skillet and fry until the whites are set but the yolks are still runny, about 3 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. To serve, divide the Sinangag (garlic fried rice) among serving plates. Top each serving with Tapa (marinated beef) and a fried egg. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious Tapsilog!
