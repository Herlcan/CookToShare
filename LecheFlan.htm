Leche Flan

Published May 10, 2024

Leche Flan is a traditional Filipino dessert influenced by Spanish history. Served during special occasions, this dessert is rich and decadent that melts in your mouth!

This classic Filipino dessert, also known as caramel custard, is rich, creamy, and indulgent.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (300ml) condensed milk
  • 1 can (370ml) evaporated milk
  • 10 egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar (for caramel)
  • 1/4 cup water

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine granulated sugar and water over medium heat. Cook, stirring constantly, until the sugar is completely dissolved and the mixture turns into a golden caramel. Pour the caramel into individual llaneras or molds, tilting to coat the bottom evenly. Set aside to cool and harden.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine condensed milk, evaporated milk, egg yolks, and vanilla extract. Whisk until well combined and smooth.
  3. Strain the mixture through a fine sieve to ensure a smooth flan texture.
  4. Pour the strained mixture into the prepared llaneras or molds with caramelized sugar.
  5. Cover each llanera with aluminum foil and steam in a steamer over low heat for about 30-40 minutes, or until the flan is set but still jiggly in the center.
  6. Remove from the steamer and let the flan cool to room temperature.
  7. Once cooled, refrigerate the flan for at least 2 hours or overnight to chill and set completely.
  8. To serve, run a knife around the edges of the llanera to loosen the flan. Place a serving plate on top of the llanera and invert it to release the flan onto the plate, revealing the caramelized sugar on top.
  9. Slice and serve the Leche Flan chilled. Enjoy!
